🛒 What you'll need — physical lab

The simulator above is for practice. If your team is also running the hands-on hydrometer lab, here's what to buy — most of it is under $25 total, and the sugar/salt/water is free from your kitchen.

Core Triple-scale hydrometer + test jar The actual instrument you're practicing to read — covers specific gravity, Brix, and ABV scales. Fermtech kit on Amazon ↗
Core 100–250 mL graduated cylinder Holds liquid deep enough for the hydrometer to float freely and clear the bottom. Test kit w/ cylinder on Amazon ↗
Core Granulated sugar Make known-concentration solutions (5%, 10%, 20% w/v) to calibrate your eye on Brix readings. Any grocery store ↗
Core Table salt Salt-water solutions sit on a different density curve than sugar — good for testing whether you understand SG vs. sugar specifically. Any grocery store ↗
Core Kitchen scale (grams) Weigh sugar/salt precisely so your % w/v solutions are accurate, not eyeballed. Digital scale on Amazon ↗
Core Distilled water Baseline reading of SG = 1.000, and avoids tap-water minerals skewing your results. Distilled water on Amazon ↗
Optional Thermometer Hydrometers are calibrated at 60°F/20°C — temperature correction is a common invitational question. ThermoPro on Amazon ↗
Optional Small funnel Cleaner transfers between the mixing container and the graduated cylinder — less mess, less waste. Kitchen/hardware store ↗
💡 Tip: Practice with both sugar and salt solutions at the same specific gravity — it's the fastest way to internalize that a hydrometer measures density, not "sugar" specifically. That distinction shows up often in invitational questions.
